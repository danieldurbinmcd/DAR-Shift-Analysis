Key Findings — Shift Analysis May 3–6
Critical Pattern
Dinner Shift Drags Every Location
Portfolio Dinner TPPH is 5.54 vs Breakfast at 6.87 — a 24% productivity drop in the same buildings with the same equipment. OEPE climbs to 222s average at Dinner vs 138s at Breakfast. This is not a traffic problem — Dinner GC is strong. It is a crew deployment and pace problem that compounds every evening.
Urgent — Late Night
Hours 22-24 Running Below 4.00 TPPH
The portfolio averages 4.87 TPPH at 10pm, 3.54 at 11pm, and 2.56 at midnight. OEPE at these hours averages 240s-265s. Hours vs need is consistently positive (+62 to +52 hrs) meaning late-night crews are over-scheduled while delivering the worst productivity. This is where scheduling cuts would have the highest return.
Best Practice Window
8am-9am is Peak Efficiency — 8.38 TPPH
Hours 8 and 9 produce the highest TPPH in the portfolio at 8.38 and 7.93 with OEPE below 145s. The portfolio runs slightly under-staffed at these hours (-44 and -3 hrs vs need). The morning manager and crew deploying at 8-9am is the model — the question every supervisor should ask is why the same crew doesn't replicate this at 5pm.
Scheduling Pattern
Lunch 11am-12pm Consistently Over-Staffed
Hours 11 and 12 run +182 hours over need across the portfolio over 4 days. TPPH drops to 5.18 and 5.45 during peak lunch traffic — the opposite of what should happen. Too many hours in the building is driving lower productivity, not higher. GMs are staffing for volume but not deploying for efficiency.
Top Performers
7562 Breakfast 8.51 TPPH — 11743 Close Behind at 8.43
7562 4th Ave leads the portfolio at Breakfast with 8.51 TPPH and OEPE of 102s — nearly 40 seconds faster than portfolio average. 11743 Whitesville runs 8.43 at Breakfast but drops to 4.95 at Dinner — the biggest shift-to-shift swing in the portfolio. Same GM, same crew, 41% productivity drop from morning to evening. That gap needs a conversation.
Needs Immediate Attention
31329 Below 4.50 Every Shift, Every Day
31329 431 North averages 4.42 at Breakfast, 3.79 at Lunch, 4.49 at Dinner — the only location that fails every shift benchmark across all 4 days. This is not a Dinner problem or a bad day — it is a systemic crew performance and deployment issue. Galisa Henderson needs a structured daily observation visit, not a weekly call.
