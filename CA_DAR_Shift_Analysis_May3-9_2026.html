Key Findings — Shift Analysis May 3–9
Portfolio Strength
All 3 Shifts Running Above 6.00 TPPH
Breakfast leads at 7.67 TPPH, Lunch at 7.57, and Dinner at 6.81. California maintains productivity above the standard threshold across every shift. Higher wages and lower turnover mean experienced crews are deployed effectively at all dayparts. The Dinner dip is present but the floor is still well above goal.
Peak Efficiency
8am–9am Delivers 8.98 TPPH
Hours 8 and 9 are the most productive in the portfolio at 8.98 and 8.92 TPPH respectively. Breakfast OEPE averages 96s portfolio-wide, comfortably under the 120s goal. The morning crew deployment in California is the benchmark — experienced crews hitting their stride at peak Breakfast traffic.
Watch — Dinner Slide
Dinner TPPH Drops to 6.81 — OEPE Climbs to 142s
While still above goal, Dinner TPPH trails Breakfast by 0.86 points and average OEPE rises to 142s. Hours 22–23 are the weakest window, with TPPH falling to 6.70 and 5.36. Late-night scheduling should be reviewed to ensure crews match demand patterns rather than running lean into a low-traffic period.
Scheduling Pattern
Hour 11 Only Hour Running Over Need
Hour 11 is the one consistent over-scheduled window at +62 hrs vs need across the portfolio. Every other productive hour is running under need. This suggests the Lunch transition is being staffed for anticipated volume that hasn’t materialized by the 11am hour. Shifting some of those hours to the 12–13 window — where GC peaks — would improve both TPPH and labor efficiency.
Best Practice
1313 Destiny Baker Leads All Shifts
1313 leads the portfolio in Breakfast TPPH with one of the best per-hour transaction rates in California. Running 416 hours under need for the week while maintaining top speed metrics shows that crew experience and deployment matter more than raw hours. Destiny Baker’s location is the clearest example of the California higher-wage model paying dividends.
Needs Attention
7433 Imelda Cortez — Only Location Consistently Over Need
7433 is the only location running over need across the week at +96 hrs, with the lowest TPPH in the portfolio at 6.19. Hourly data shows consistent over-staffing at Dinner. The extra hours are not producing higher GC or better OEPE. Ray Marez needs to review the scheduling structure at this location against actual hourly demand patterns.
